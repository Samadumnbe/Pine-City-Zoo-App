LION

The lion (Panthera leo) is one of the five big cats in the genus Panthera and a member of the family Felidae. The commonly use term African lion collectively denote the several subspecies found in Africa. With some males exceeding 250 kg (550 Ib) in weight, it is the second-largest living cat after the tiger. Wild lions currently exist in sub-Saharan Africa and in Asia (where endangered remnant population resides in Gir forests National Park in India) while other types of lions have disappeared from North Africa and Southwest Asia in historic times. Until the late Pleistocene, about 10 000 years ago, the lion was the most widespread large land mamal after humans. They were found in most of Africa, across Eurasia from western Europe to India, and in the Americas from the Yukon to Peru.

The lion is a vulnerable species, having seen a major population decline in its African range of 30 to 50% per two decades during the second half of the twentieth century. Lion populations are uneaten outside designated reserves and national parks. Although the cause of the decline is not fully understood, habitat loss and conflicts with humans are currently the greatest causes of concern.
